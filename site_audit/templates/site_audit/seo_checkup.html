Core Vitals are now a part of Google’s search algorithm and can positively or negatively impact your keyword rankings. Low Core Vitals scores can also negatively impact your visitors’ overall experience on your website. Google has assigned different weights (i.e. importance) to each Core Vital metric. That said, if you score very bad for any Core Vital it is always going to be a top priority to fix even if it isn’t the most important Core Vital.

How Google generally weighs the importance of each main Core Vital

  • Total Blocking Time (TBT) 30 %
  • Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) 25 %
  • Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS) 15 %
  • First Contentful Paint (FCP) 10 %
  • Speed Index (SI) 10 %
  • Time to Interact (TTI) 10 %

What is TBT?

TBT measures the total amount of time that a page is blocked from responding to user input, such as mouse clicks, screen taps, or keyboard presses.

What is a good TBT score?

If your TBT is 200 milliseconds or less your website is considered fast.

What is LCP?

This metric corresponds to the first impression users have of how fast your site loads. It reports the render time of the largest image or text block visible to a visitor, relative to when the page first started loading. A fast LCP helps let the user know that the page is useful.

What is a good LCP score?

To provide a good user experience, sites should strive to have Largest Contentful Paint of 2.5 seconds or less.

What is CLS?

We’ve all experienced CLS and it can be annoying! Essentially, CLS measures how much things shift around on a website page while it is loading. For example, a visitor might start reading your “About Us” page and then all of a sudden, a big image loads and it moves the text down and they lose their place. Even worse, a visitor may be about to push a “More Info” button, but it gets pushed down and instead they end up clicking a “Buy Now” button. A low CLS score is important if you want to provide your visitors with a good browsing experience.

What is a good CLS?

To provide a good user experience, sites should strive to have a CLS score of 0.1 or less.

What is FCP?

FCP measures how long it takes a visitor’s browser to render the first image, text, or non-white element on your page after they land on it. This needs to be fast, as it lets a visitor know the page is starting to load. Until FCP, the visitor doesn’t see anything!

What is a good FCP score?

FCP needs to occur in under 1.8 seconds in order for your website to be considered good!

What is Speed Index?

Speed Index measures how quickly content is visually displayed to your website users during page load. Basically, a video is taken of your page loading in the browser and then your score is computed based on the speed of visual progression between frames.

What is a good Speed Index score?

If your Speed Index is 3.4 seconds or less your website is considered fast.

What is TTI?

TTI measures how long it takes a page to become fully interactive for a visitor. A page is considered fully interactive when:

  • The page displays useful content, which is measured by the First Contentful Paint
  • Event handlers are registered for most visible page elements
  • The page responds to user interactions within 50 milliseconds
