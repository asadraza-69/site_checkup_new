Keywords

Discover where your competitors are getting their traffic from! Also, find new keywords that you can use in articles and blogs on your website.

Backlinks

Find new backlink opportunities for your website. Backlinks from other websites can generate more traffic to your website and also increase your website authority.

How to use this tool

  • Enter a competitor’s website into We

    Try to stick with competitors that look the most like your business. Even though Amazon or Wikipedia might rank above you they are probably not very useful competitors to analyze for keyword ideas.

  • Build a list of potential keywords

    Look at the related keywords and volume. Could you also use any of these in the title and content of an article or blog post?

  • Start writing!

    We suggest making your articles at least 1000 words long. Write naturally (only using the keyword occasionally) and when you have a great article post it on your website.

  • Scroll through their backlinks.

    The higher the authority the more important they are to your competitors and likely to you! Focus on the backlinks that are in your industry and have something to do with your services and/or products.

  • Visit the websites that look interesting.

    Find where they link to your competitor. Is there a reason they might add your website link on the same page or a new page? Email them with the best reasons for why they should also link to your website. We’ve found this takes time but even if only 1 out of 10 adds your website you will see your traffic and website authority benefiting!

After you've found some new keywords, search for them in Google and see what types of articles already rank well.

This should give you an idea of what to write about. For instance, if you Google “raw honey” you will see a lot of articles in the top 10 positions talking about the “benefits” of “raw honey”. It is a good idea to follow their example! Also localize your article if you are focused on a specific city or area. For instance, it is easier to rank for “How to find a dog walker in New York City” than the national term “Dog walker”.

Keep a spreadsheet of all the backlink opportunities you have found and include any notes (i.e., when you email them).

Backlinking is one of the most important things you can do but it also takes significant time. Plan on devoting at least a few hours a month to this or have an employee/family member do it for you.
